PAYPAL PAYMENT EXPERIENCE

PROBLEM

Today, large enterprise merchants struggle with managing employee access to PayPal accounts. The problem with managing employee access to PayPal accounts manifests from the following problems that exist with the current solution: multiple PayPal credentials per LE employee, painful onboarding or password management.

SOLUTION

Single Sign On (SSO) allows a user to utilize their corporate credentials to log into PayPal Inc. services instead of a PayPal Inc service-specific username and password. It is a common requirement for Large Enterprise merchants who want to securely manage hundreds or thousands of their users' access to multiple third-party services.

TARGET MARKET

The primary target audience consists of large enterprise merchants who need to manage employee access to PayPal services. These organizations typically have hundreds or thousands of employees requiring access to PayPal accounts, and they need a secure, efficient way to manage these permissions.

The solution is particularly valuable for IT administrators and security teams who are responsible for maintaining secure access controls while ensuring smooth operations for their employees.

USE CASE 1

Error Flow: Organization Not Set Up for SSO

When a user attempts to log in with SSO but their organization hasn't been configured for SSO access, they encounter a clear error message explaining the situation and providing next steps for resolution.

USE CASE 2

Success Flow: Log in with Cookies

When a user successfully logs in with their corporate credentials, the system creates and stores cookies for future sessions, enabling seamless access to PayPal services without repeated authentication.

USE CASE 3

Error Flow: User Not Set Up for SSO

When a user attempts to log in with SSO but their individual account hasn't been configured for SSO access, they receive a clear error message with instructions on how to set up their account for SSO authentication.
